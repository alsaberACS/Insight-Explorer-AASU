Course Learning Outcomes (CLOs)
By the end of this course, students should be able to:
Explain the role of data and evidence in decision-making across society, organizations, and professional disciplines.
Describe the data analytics lifecycle, from problem identification and data collection to analysis, interpretation, and communication.
Identify and classify different types, sources, and formats of data (structured, semi-structured, and unstructured).
Assess the quality, credibility, reliability, and validity of data from different sources.
Formulate analytical questions and identify the variables required to answer real-world problems.
Collect, import, clean, transform, and prepare datasets for analysis using modern analytical tools.
Apply exploratory data analysis (EDA) techniques to summarize and understand datasets.
Create effective charts, dashboards, and data visualizations that communicate insights clearly and accurately.
Demonstrate an understanding of fundamental statistical concepts, including distributions, sampling, variability, probability, and uncertainty.
Explain the principles of descriptive and inferential statistics and their role in evidence-based decision-making.
Understand the concept of statistical hypothesis testing, including null and alternative hypotheses, significance levels, p-values, confidence intervals, effect sizes, and statistical power.
Select appropriate statistical methods for different types of research questions and data.
Interpret the results of common statistical analyses correctly and communicate their practical implications.
Understand the concepts of correlation, causation, prediction, and inference, and distinguish among them appropriately.
Explain the principles of predictive and explanatory modeling, including regression, classification, clustering, and introductory machine learning techniques.
Evaluate the performance and limitations of analytical models using appropriate evaluation metrics.
Analyze data using artificial intelligence tools and modern analytical software to improve efficiency and productivity.
Critically evaluate AI-generated analyses, recognizing potential errors, biases, hallucinations, and limitations.
Recognize misleading graphs, misinformation, statistical fallacies, and common sources of bias in data interpretation.
Apply ethical principles, privacy considerations, and responsible AI practices throughout the data analysis process.
Communicate analytical findings effectively through written reports, presentations, dashboards, and data storytelling tailored to both technical and non-technical audiences.
Collaborate effectively in multidisciplinary teams to solve real-world problems using data.
Complete an end-to-end data analytics project by collecting, analyzing, interpreting, and presenting evidence-based recommendations relevant to the student's field of study.
